Common questions

What property managers ask before they switch.

Plain answers to the four questions that come up most. If yours isn't on the list, email or call. Same-day reply, no sales pressure.

What insurance do you carry?

Professional Indemnity £5 million. Public Liability £10 million. Employers' Liability £10 million. Sourced via Hencilla Canworth, the Fire Industry Association's insurance partner. Documentation provided to every customer at engagement. Most fire alarm contractors at our scale carry less. We carry more because the buildings we look after are worth it.

What if your engineer is on holiday or off-shift?

Same engineer every visit is the default, not a contractual ringfence. Planned holidays, we schedule your visits around. Unplanned absence, we have backup engineers from the FIA network with equivalent qualifications. You'll always be told who's coming and why, before they arrive. The single-engineer risk you're imagining is one we've already solved.

Can I switch from my current contractor mid-contract?

Almost always yes. Most fire alarm maintenance contracts allow termination on 30 to 90 days' written notice. We'll point out the clauses you should look at and time the switchover so coverage is uninterrupted. The contractor you're leaving doesn't get a say. You do.

How fast can you respond to an emergency?

Working hours (8am-6pm, weekdays): same-day where possible, next working day at the latest. Out-of-hours, weekends, true emergency: target response within 4 hours. The 24/7 line is answered by the engineer on call, not a call-centre. £225 per standard callout, £400 out-of-hours.
